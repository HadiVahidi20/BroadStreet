Our Story

Broadstreet Rugby Football Club was founded in 1929 and has been at the heart of Coventry rugby ever since.

For nearly a century, we have been developing players of all ages, from mini rugby through to senior teams. Our club is built on the values of teamwork, respect, and community.

Based at Ivor Preece Field in Binley Woods, we provide excellent facilities for players and supporters alike. Our clubhouse offers a welcoming environment for the whole rugby family.

Today, Broadstreet RFC continues to thrive with multiple senior teams, a vibrant youth section, and a growing women's programme. We remain committed to our founding principles while embracing the future of rugby.

Our History

1929

Club Founded

Broadstreet RFC was established by a group of rugby enthusiasts in Coventry, laying the foundation for what would become one of the city's most respected clubs.

1950s

Post-War Growth

Following World War II, the club experienced significant growth, establishing itself as a competitive force in local rugby leagues.

1970s

New Facilities

The club moved to its current home at Ivor Preece Field, named after one of our most dedicated members and benefactors.

1990s

Youth Development

Launch of our comprehensive mini and junior rugby programme, nurturing the next generation of players.

2010s

Clubhouse Renovation

Major investment in clubhouse facilities, creating a modern venue for events while preserving our traditional character.

2020s

Women's Rugby

Establishment of our women's section, ensuring rugby at Broadstreet is truly inclusive for all.
